Catwoman: The Feline Fatale!

© DC Comics. Drawn by Jim Balent

Character Profile

Selina Kyle, a classic femme fatale, often straddles the line between good and evil.

Initially, in Batman #62 (1950), Selina's criminal career began due to amnesia. This origin was later redefined in The Brave and the Bold #197, where she was depicted as turning to crime after escaping an abusive marriage.

In modern continuity, Selina takes up crime to survive after witnessing the tragic death of her mother, Maria Kyle and soon after becoming an orphan.

Other storylines, such as Catwoman (New 52), When in Rome, and Catwoman by Tini Howard (do not reommend!) have toyed with the idea of Selina's parentage, suggesting ties to mob families.

Recommended Catwoman Comics

Ed Brubaker's run was character-defining, firmly establishing Selina as an anti-heroine and also giving her a new look that's still used today. It is my one of my favorite comics, though I overall prefer her characterization in Jim Balents run!

If you choose to read any of these I would recommend Brubaker's run

  2. Catwoman by Jim Balent Book Two

  5. Catwoman Vol 5:Valley of the Shadow of Death by Ram V
