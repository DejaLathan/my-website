© DC Comics. Drawn by Jim Balent

Character Profile

Selina Kyle the femme fatale who often straddles the line between good and evil.

Selina Kyle originally began her crime career due to amnesia in Batman #62 1950. Then this origin was changed into her having turned to a life of crime after an abusive marriage in Brave and the Bold #197

In the modern age Selina Kyle turns to crime to support herself after witnessing the death of her mother - Maria Kyle.

Other origins like Catwoman New 52, When in Rome, and currently in DC Rebirth toy with her parentage being italian mob bosses

Comics to read for Catwoman

Catwoman is a versatile character with a loose moral code. While oftentimes more villaness in older batman comics, she has been portrayed as being an anti-hero with morals that conflict with Batman

Brubaker's run was character defining and firmly planted Selina as an anti-heroine. It's my favorite comic - though overall I prefer the characterization in Balent's run!
